# Annual Board Report Template

## A Structured Framework for Board Reporting

*Governance Toolkit · Reference Series · Richard Porter · 2026*

*This template provides a structure for the annual board report — the internal document that captures the organization’s year in a format that serves governance, not marketing. It is distinct from a public annual report (which serves donors and community) and from financial statements (which serve auditors and regulators). The board report serves the board: what happened, what it means, and what comes next.*

-----

## Purpose

Most boards receive one of two things at year-end: nothing, or a stack of financial statements. Neither serves governance. Financial statements show where the money went. They do not show whether the organization achieved its mission, whether governance was effective, whether the people (staff, volunteers, clients) were well-served, or whether the organization is positioned for the year ahead.

The annual board report fills that gap. It is a single document, prepared by the executive with board input, that gives the board a complete picture of the year across six dimensions: mission delivery, financial stewardship, people, governance health, risk, and strategic outlook. Each section is designed to answer the question: “What does the board need to know to govern effectively going forward?”

**Audience:** The board. Not the public, not donors, not funders. If the organization also produces a public annual report, the board report provides the source material and honest assessment behind it.

**Timing:** Prepared within 60 days of fiscal year-end. Presented at the first board meeting of the new fiscal year.

**Length:** 8–12 pages. If it’s longer, it’s trying to do too much. If it’s shorter, it’s not doing enough.

-----

## How to Use This Template

Each section below contains the section purpose, the questions it should answer, a fill-in structure, and guidance notes. Replace the italicized placeholder text with your organization’s information. Delete the guidance notes before presenting to the board.

Sections marked with an asterisk (*) connect directly to other toolkit components. Data from those tools can feed directly into the corresponding report section.

## Assembly Guide

How to assemble the annual board report using this template and existing toolkit data:

|Report Section                      |Data Source                                                                                                                      |
|------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Section 1: Executive Summary**    |Written by executive after all other sections are complete. Summarizes the whole report.                                         |
|**Section 2: Mission Delivery**     |Program data, outcome metrics, strategic plan progress. If Carver Ends are defined, report against Ends.                         |
|**Section 3: Financial Stewardship**|Year-end financial statements, audit report, budget-to-actual comparison.                                                        |
|**Section 4: People**               |People Pulse data (Pulse Check scores, Stay Interview themes, Departure Debrief findings). HR records for headcount and turnover.|
|**Section 5: Governance Health**    |Governance Audit results (if conducted), board attendance records, executive evaluation summary, corrective action plan status.  |
|**Section 6: Risk Register**        |Updated from prior year. Executive identifies new risks; board reviews and validates.                                            |
|**Section 7: Strategic Outlook**    |Strategic plan, executive assessment, environmental scan. Board-facing decisions identified by executive and Board Chair.        |

-----

## Toolkit Connections

- **People Pulse:** Section 4 (People) draws directly from Pulse Check aggregate scores, Stay Interview themes, and Departure Debrief patterns. If People Pulse is active, this section writes itself.
- **Governance Audit:** Section 5 (Governance Health) reports the audit’s findings, Met/Partial/Not Met counts, and corrective action status. The audit IS the governance section’s data source.
- **Policy Governance (Carver):** If Carver is adopted, Section 2 (Mission Delivery) reports against defined Ends. Section 5 includes Executive Limitation compliance status. The report structure aligns naturally with Carver’s monitoring framework.
- **Change Management (Kotter):** Any change initiatives in progress should be referenced in the relevant section (usually Section 7: Strategic Outlook) with current Kotter step identified.
- **Merger & Affiliation Readiness:** If a merger or partnership is under consideration or in progress, Section 7 should include the readiness assessment findings and integration status.

-----

*The annual board report is not a celebration document. It is an honest accounting of what the organization accomplished, what it struggled with, and where it is headed. A board that reads this report should feel informed, not impressed. If the report makes the organization look perfect, it is not honest enough. If it overwhelms the board with data, it is not focused enough.*

*The best annual report is the one that tells the board something it didn’t already know.*
