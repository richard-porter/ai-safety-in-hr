## Why This Exists

Most small organizations hire the way they do everything else: informally, urgently, and without a system. The result is predictable: the wrong person in the role, no way to evaluate them, no paper trail to explain the decision, and no framework for the 90-day or annual review.

This tool provides the system. It is cause-agnostic — the methodology works for any role at any small organization. The specific competencies, questions, and scoring criteria are customized per engagement. The architecture is fixed.

**What this tool is:** A repeatable hiring methodology with seven stages, from job description through annual review, using a single competency framework that persists across all stages.

**What this tool is not:** A job description library, a salary survey, or an HR compliance manual. Those are inputs to this tool, not products of it.

-----

## The Architecture: One Framework, Seven Stages

The core design principle: the same competencies evaluated at every stage, with increasing depth. No moving goalposts. No surprise criteria at the final interview that weren’t present at the resume screen. The candidate sees the same values from posting to annual review.

|Stage 1             |Stage 2             |Stage 3            |Stage 4            |Stage 5          |Stage 6             |Stage 7          |
|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------|
|**Gate + Score**    |**Phone Screen**    |**Panel Interview**|**Final Interview**|**Trial**        |**90-Day Review**   |**Annual Review**|
|Paper review        |20–30 min           |60–75 min, STAR    |45 min, trust      |3–4 hrs, optional|Formal eval         |Renewal decision |
|Can we see evidence?|Do they sound right?|Can they prove it? |Do we trust them?  |Can they do it?  |Are they delivering?|Should we renew? |

## Step 0: Build the Competency Framework

Before any posting goes live, define the role using the 4-Step Competency Model (Mr. Wolff v2.0). This produces the competency framework that powers every subsequent stage.

**Required inputs:**

- **Role Profile** (from 4-Step Model, Step 1): Key accountabilities, leadership competencies, technical competencies, key experiences, key relationships.
- **Core Competencies** (4–8): The evaluable categories that will be scored at every stage. Derived from the role profile. Each must be observable, scorable, and relevant from resume through annual review.
- **Must-Have Gate** (3–5 items): Binary pass/fail requirements. If a candidate fails any gate item, they do not advance regardless of competency scores.
- **Universal Scoring Scale** (1–5): Consistent across all stages. Simple enough for volunteer or non-HR interviewers to use without training.

### Universal Scoring Scale

|Score|Label               |What It Means                                                                                                       |
|-----|--------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**1**|Poor Fit            |No relevant experience or evidence. Answers are generic, disconnected from the role, or raise concerns.             |
|**2**|Below Expectations  |Minimal experience. Some awareness but significant gaps. Would require substantial development.                     |
|**3**|Meets Expectations  |Solid, capable, aligned. Clear examples of relevant experience. Could do the job with normal support.               |
|**4**|Exceeds Expectations|Strong skills with clear added value. Brings experience directly transferable to the organization’s specific needs. |
|**5**|Exceptional         |Outstanding fit. Brings transformative potential. Existing networks, proven track record, or rare skill combination.|

*Rule of thumb: A “3” across the board is a hirable candidate. You’re looking for someone who averages 3+ with at least one 4 or 5 in the competency most critical to the role’s primary function.*

## Stage 1: Resume Screening (Gate + Score + Tier)

**Purpose:** Efficiently sort applications so the hiring decision-maker only phone-screens viable candidates.

**Who:** One person with HR or hiring experience. Not the full group.

**Method:** Gate first (binary pass/fail), then score (1–5 per competency), then tier.

### Must-Have Gate (Template)

|Requirement                       |Pass / Fail    |Evidence                               |
|----------------------------------|---------------|---------------------------------------|
|[Must-have #1 — from role profile]|               |                                       |
|[Must-have #2]                    |               |                                       |
|[Must-have #3]                    |               |                                       |
|[Must-have #4]                    |               |                                       |
|**Gate Result**                   |**Pass / Fail**|*If FAIL on any item, do not continue.*|

### Competency Scoring (Template)

|  |Competency    |Score  |Evidence from Resume & Cover Letter|
|--|--------------|-------|-----------------------------------|
|C1|[Competency 1]|       |                                   |
|C2|[Competency 2]|       |                                   |
|C3|[Competency 3]|       |                                   |
|C4|[Competency 4]|       |                                   |
|C5|[Competency 5]|       |                                   |
|C6|[Competency 6]|       |                                   |
|  |**Total**     |**/30**|                                   |

### Tiering

|Tier                 |Criteria                                    |Action                                               |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
|**A — Strong**       |All gate items pass + competency score 20–30|Phone screen. Priority scheduling.                   |
|**B — Possible**     |All gate items pass + competency score 15–19|Phone screen if Tier A pool is thin. Worth exploring.|
|**C — Does Not Meet**|Any gate fail or competency score < 15      |Polite decline. Do not advance.                      |

**Deliverable:** One-page screening summary per candidate. Tier A candidates on top. Deliver shortlist to hiring decision-maker.

## Stage 2: Phone Screen (20–30 Minutes)

**Purpose:** Confirm role fit, logistical alignment, and initial impression. Thin the pool from 3–5 to 2–3 finalists.

**Who:** Hiring decision-maker + 1 other person (board member, senior volunteer, trusted colleague).

**Method:** Same competency framework. One question per competency. Same 1–5 scale. Ask all candidates the same core questions.

### Question Design Principle

Each question should map to one competency and have a clear “listen for” instruction. At phone screen depth, you’re testing whether the candidate sounds right, not whether they can prove it — that’s Stage 3.

**Template per question:**

- **Competency:** [C1–C6]
- **Question:** [Open-ended, specific to role context]
- **Listen for:** [What a strong answer sounds like vs. a weak one]

### Phone Screen Scoring

Same competency table as Stage 1, plus an Overall Impression row (/35 total). Add a recommendation: Strong Yes / Yes / Maybe / No.

**Decision rule:** Strong Yes and Yes advance to panel. Maybe holds for comparison. No does not advance.

-----

## Stage 3: Panel Interview (60–75 Minutes, STAR Format)

**Purpose:** Deep competency assessment using behavioral questions. This is where you find out if they can prove it, not just talk about it.

**Who:** Panel of 3–4. Include at least one external perspective (partner organization, key community member, longtime stakeholder) for cultural fit lens. Non-voting is fine.

**Method:** STAR-format questions (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Each panelist scores independently within 24 hours. One person synthesizes.

### STAR Method Reference

|Element      |What to Listen For                                                                    |
|-------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Situation**|Specific context. Not hypothetical. “Tell me about a time” not “What would you do if.”|
|**Task**     |What was this person’s specific responsibility? Not the team’s. Theirs.               |
|**Action**   |What did they actually do? Probe with: “What was your specific role?”                 |
|**Result**   |What happened? What did they learn? Probe with: “What would you change?”              |

### Question Design for Panel

One STAR question per competency, plus one scenario question that tests two competencies simultaneously. The scenario should be drawn from a real operational challenge the organization has faced or will face.

**Universal follow-up probes** (use with every STAR question):

- “What was your specific role?”
- “What was the hardest part?”
- “What would you change?”

### Panel Scoring

Same competency table. Each panelist scores independently. Synthesizer compiles scores, identifies convergence and divergence, flags any competency where scores differ by 2+ points between panelists.

**Additional panel outputs:**

- Top 3 strengths observed
- Concerns or development areas
- Best quote or example from the interview
- Cultural fit: High / Medium / Low (with rationale)
- Recommendation: Strong Yes / Yes / Maybe / No

-----

## Stage 4: Final Interview (45 Minutes)

**Purpose:** Strategic vision, trust, and final gut check. Not a re-run of the panel. The decision-makers are answering one question: do we trust this person to represent our organization?

**Who:** Full hiring authority (board, ownership, leadership group). The synthesizer provides a one-page candidate summary (all prior scores, strengths, concerns) before the meeting.

**Method:** Same competency framework. Same 1–5 scale. Questions focused on vision, accountability, and fit. Decision by majority.

### Recommended Final Interview Questions (Cause-Agnostic)

- Where do you see this organization in three years, and what’s your role in getting us there?
- What’s the first thing you’d do in your first week?
- How would you handle a situation where a long-tenured person disagrees with a change you want to make?
- What does accountability look like to you in a role where no one is watching your hours?
- What questions do you have for us?

**Decision:** Hire / Do Not Hire / Need More Information. Majority required.

## Stage 5: Paid Trial (Optional, 3–4 Hours)

**Purpose:** You learn more in 3 hours of real work than in 3 interviews. Before the full offer, the top finalist completes a small real task at the role’s hourly rate.

**Method:** Assign 2–3 tasks that mirror actual job duties. Must be compensated. Must be real work, not make-work. Evaluate with a written assessment against the competency framework.

### Trial Design Principles

- **Real:** The tasks should be things the role will actually do. Draft a communication. Build a 30-day priority list. Analyze a real dataset.
- **Bounded:** 3–4 hours maximum. Clear deliverables. Deadline stated.
- **Paid:** At the role’s hourly rate. This is work, not an audition.
- **Evaluated:** Written assessment using the competency framework. Not vibes.

**Stop if:** The trial is being used to extract free labor or to avoid making a decision. The trial confirms a hire, not postpones one.

## Stages 6 & 7: Performance Reviews

The same competency framework carries through into performance management. The shift: instead of evaluating potential, you’re evaluating performance against agreed-upon targets.

### 90-Day Review (Stage 6)

**Purpose:** Formal evaluation and contract continuation decision point.

**Method:** Same six competencies scored 1–5. Each competency now has a specific target derived from the job description and onboarding plan. Evidence column shows actual performance against target.

**Decision:** Continue / Continue with Conditions / Terminate.

**Critical:** The 90-day review should feel like a natural continuation of the hiring process, not a surprise. If the competencies and scoring were transparent during hiring, the new hire already knows what success looks like.

### Annual Review (Stage 7)

**Purpose:** Comprehensive evaluation. Contract renewal decision.

**Method:** Same competency framework plus quantitative metrics. Metrics should be established mutually within the first 30 days, not imposed retroactively.

**The 12-month validation question:** Does this role generate more value than it costs? For revenue-generating roles: does the role raise or generate more than its total compensation cost? For operational roles: has the organization’s capacity, compliance, or sustainability measurably improved?

**Decision:** Renew / Renew with Modifications / Do Not Renew.

## Interviewer Guide (For Non-HR Interviewers)

Most people on a hiring panel have never conducted a structured interview. This guide prevents the most common mistakes. Distribute before Stage 2.

|Do                                           |Don’t                                                 |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
|Use the scoring rubric for every candidate   |Wing it or rely on gut feeling alone                  |
|Ask the same core questions to each candidate|Ask personal questions (family, age, health, religion)|
|Take notes during the interview              |Interrupt or finish the candidate’s sentences         |
|Listen 70%, talk 30%                         |Dominate the conversation                             |
|Describe the role and challenges honestly    |Oversell or minimize the difficulties                 |
|Allow comfortable silence for thinking       |Rush to fill pauses                                   |
|Respect confidentiality                      |Discuss candidates outside the hiring team            |

### Legal Safety

**Never ask about:** Age, marital or family status, religion, health or disability, politics, personal finances, race or ethnicity, pregnancy, national origin.

**If personal information is volunteered:** Listen politely, steer back to job-related topics, do not note it on the scoring form.

**Documentation rule:** Use only the official scoring rubric. Notes should be factual, behavior-based, and confidential. The scoring forms create a paper trail that demonstrates fair, structured decision-making.

## Process Management

### No-Contact Policy

During active candidacy, hiring team members should not independently contact candidates outside the structured interview process. Well-meaning individual outreach creates mixed signals, unequal access, and legal exposure.

### Timeline Management

A typical hiring process for a key role runs 10–14 weeks from posting to start date:

|Phase                      |Duration |Key Actions                                                              |
|---------------------------|---------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Pre-Launch**             |2–3 weeks|Finalize JD, set up infrastructure, prepare materials, brief interviewers|
|**Applications Open**      |3–4 weeks|Post, promote, monitor inbox, boost at midpoint                          |
|**Screening & Interviews** |4–6 weeks|Screen, phone, panel, final interview, references, background check      |
|**Offer & Onboarding Prep**|1–2 weeks|Extend offer, execute agreement, set up systems, prepare Day 1 materials |

### Materials Checklist

**Before posting:**

- Finalized job description
- Competency framework with scoring scale
- Must-have gate criteria
- Resume screening summary template
- Phone screen questions with listen-for notes
- Panel interview questions (STAR format)
- Scoring rubric for all interviewers
- Interviewer guide for non-HR participants
- Auto-reply for application inbox

**Before offer:**

- Offer letter template
- Employment agreement template
- Background check process
- One-page org fact sheet for candidates

**Before Day 1:**

- 30-60-90 day plan template
- System access and password handoff protocol
- Key contact and stakeholder directory
- Meeting and reporting schedule
- 90-day review date on calendar

## Signal Recognition

|Green Flags                                                  |Red Flags                                            |
|-------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
|Asks thoughtful questions about the organization’s challenges|Vague answers that could apply to any organization   |
|Gives specific examples with measurable results              |Overly focused on title, authority, or personal brand|
|Shows knowledge of the local context and community           |Speaks negatively about past employers or supervisors|
|Expresses realistic excitement about the role’s constraints  |Avoids discussing key competency areas               |
|Acknowledges what they don’t know and asks how they’d learn  |Claims expertise in everything without evidence      |
|References align with interview performance                  |References are all personal, not professional        |
