# Organizational Due Diligence Framework

## People, Culture, and Operations Assessment for Acquisitions and Mergers

*Sovereign Thinking Tools · Enterprise Extension · Richard Porter · 2026*

*Adapted from 25+ years of enterprise HR due diligence practice across aerospace, financial services, and healthcare sectors. This is the general-purpose version of the Nonprofit Merger & Affiliation Readiness Assessment — reframed for corporate acquisitions, private equity transactions, and business combinations where the acquiring organization has HR or operational leadership involved in the diligence process.*

*This is a discovery instrument: it surfaces what you need to know before you commit, not after.*

-----

## Why This Document Exists

Most M&A due diligence focuses on financials, legal structure, and intellectual property. The organizational infrastructure — people, compensation, benefits, employment practices, culture, and operational systems — is typically underexamined in the pre-close period and over-discovered in the post-close period.

The result is predictable: acquirers inherit liabilities they didn’t price, retention risks they didn’t model, and cultural incompatibilities they didn’t assess. The people problems that surface in the first 90 days post-close are almost always problems that existed before the deal closed. This framework is designed to find them first.

**What this is:** A structured discovery instrument for organizational due diligence, covering eight domains. It can be completed by an HR leader, an operations executive, or an external advisor. It is designed to complement — not replace — legal and financial due diligence.

**What this is not:** A legal review, a financial audit, or a compliance certification. Each domain will surface items requiring legal counsel review. Flag them; don’t resolve them yourself.

**Scale:** Designed for transactions involving 10–500 employees in the target organization. For larger transactions, expand the question set and add functional specialists per domain.

-----

## How to Use This Framework

**Assign domain leads:** Match domain leads to expertise. HR leads Domains 4 and 6. Finance leads Domain 3. Legal leads Domains 2 and 7. Operations leads Domain 5. Integration lead owns Domain 8.

**Discovery sessions:** Plan two to four sessions with target leadership, 90 minutes each, covering two to three domains per session. Conduct sessions before exclusivity or Letter of Intent if possible. Definitely before close.

**Document everything:** Record responses, note evidence provided, flag unanswered questions. Unanswered questions are findings — they indicate either a gap in the target’s documentation or a reluctance to disclose.

**Rate each domain:**

- **GREEN — Clean:** No material gaps, risks, or unknowns. Standard integration planning applies.
- **YELLOW — Manageable:** Issues identified that can be addressed in integration planning or purchase agreement reps and warranties. Price or structure adjustment may be warranted.
- **RED — Material:** Issues that are significant enough to affect deal valuation, require specific contractual protection, or represent potential deal-breakers. Escalate to deal team.

## Domain 1: Strategic Fit & Business Rationale

*Establish whether the combination makes strategic sense before examining operational details. Strategic misalignment produces dysfunctional organizations regardless of operational compatibility.*

|#  |Discovery Question                                                                                       |Response / Evidence|
|---|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|1.1|What is the target’s core value proposition? How does it complement or conflict with the acquirer’s?     |                   |
|1.2|What markets, customers, or geographies does the target serve that the acquirer does not?                |                   |
|1.3|What is the target’s competitive positioning? Where does it win, and why?                                |                   |
|1.4|Why is the target’s ownership pursuing this transaction? What problem does it solve for them?            |                   |
|1.5|Are there key customers, contracts, or relationships that are change-of-control sensitive?               |                   |
|1.6|Are there products, services, or markets the target is committed to that the acquirer would want to exit?|                   |
|1.7|Has the target previously explored or attempted a sale or merger? What happened?                         |                   |
|1.8|What is the target’s view of what happens to its brand, products, and identity post-close?               |                   |

## Domain 2: Corporate Governance & Legal Structure

*Understand the target’s legal and governance infrastructure. This domain surfaces structural liabilities and obligations that transfer with the entity.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                                                      |Response / Evidence|
|----|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|2.1 |What is the target’s legal structure? (Corporation, LLC, S-Corp, partnership, subsidiary) State of incorporation?                                       |                   |
|2.2 |Provide the cap table, shareholder agreements, and any outstanding warrants, options, or convertible instruments.                                       |                   |
|2.3 |Are there any board observer rights, consent rights, or protective provisions that affect the transaction?                                              |                   |
|2.4 |Are there any pending or recent legal actions, regulatory proceedings, or government investigations?                                                    |                   |

|2.8 |Has the target undergone any government audits, tax examinations, or enforcement actions in the past five years?                                        |                   |
|2.9 |Are there any consent requirements — from customers, vendors, landlords, or regulators — triggered by the transaction?                                  |                   |
|2.10|Does the target hold any licenses, permits, or certifications that must be maintained or transferred?                                                   |                   |

## Domain 3: Financial Position & Liabilities

*Assess financial health, sustainability, and the liabilities that transfer. This domain is the primary source of valuation-relevant findings in organizational due diligence.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                 |Response / Evidence|
|----|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|3.1 |Provide audited financial statements for the past three years and the most recent management accounts.             |                   |
|3.2 |What is the current cash position, working capital, and burn rate (if pre-profitable)?                             |                   |
|3.3 |What is the revenue breakdown by customer, product, and geography? Concentration risks?                            |                   |
|3.4 |What are the gross margin economics by product or service line?                                                    |                   |
|3.5 |Are there any off-balance-sheet obligations, contingent liabilities, or deferred revenue arrangements?             |                   |
|3.6 |What is the accounts receivable aging? Are there any material bad debt provisions or disputed receivables?         |                   |
|3.7 |Are there any outstanding loans, lines of credit, or debt instruments that must be addressed at close?             |                   |
|3.8 |What are the deferred compensation, bonus, or equity vesting events triggered by or accelerated by the transaction?|                   |
|3.9 |Are there any earn-out arrangements from prior acquisitions still in effect?                                       |                   |
|3.10|What is the normalized EBITDA? What one-time or non-recurring items are included in reported results?              |                   |

## Domain 4: People & Employment

*The domain that produces the most post-close surprises. Compensation, benefits, employment practices, and workforce risks must be assessed before close, not discovered after.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                                                           |Response / Evidence|
|----|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|4.1 |Provide a headcount summary by function, level, location, and employment type (FT/PT, exempt/non-exempt, contractor).                                        |                   |
|4.2 |Provide the organizational chart and reporting relationships. Where are the single points of failure?                                                        |                   |
|4.3 |Provide the compensation structure: base salary ranges, bonus structure, commission plans, and equity grants by role.                                        |                   |
|4.4 |What benefits are offered? (Health, dental, vision, retirement match, PTO, parental leave, other) Provide plan summaries and total benefit cost per employee.|                   |
|4.5 |What is the retirement plan structure? (401k type, match, vesting schedule, plan assets and liabilities) Are there any defined benefit pension obligations?  |                   |
|4.6 |What is the voluntary attrition rate for the past two years? Which roles or levels have the highest turnover?                                                |                   |
|4.7 |Are there any pending or recent employment claims, EEOC charges, wage and hour complaints, or employment litigation?                                         |                   |
|4.8 |Provide the employee handbook and key HR policies. When were they last updated? Are they legally current in all operating jurisdictions?                     |                   |
|4.9 |How are employee records maintained? What HRIS is in use? Who has access?                                                                                    |                   |
|4.10|Are there any contractor or gig worker arrangements? How many? What roles? Have classification risks been assessed?                                          |                   |
|4.11|What leave obligations apply? (FMLA, state-specific paid leave, ADA accommodations currently active)                                                         |                   |
|4.12|Are there any employment agreements, non-competes, non-solicits, or confidentiality agreements? Which are enforceable in the relevant jurisdictions?         |                   |
|4.13|What is the background check and pre-employment screening process? Are there roles with regulatory screening requirements?                                   |                   |
|4.14|I-9 and E-Verify compliance: when was the last audit? Any known issues?                                                                                      |                   |
|4.15|Are there any change-of-control provisions, retention agreements, or severance obligations triggered by the transaction?                                     |                   |
|4.16|Which employees are flight risks? Who are the key people the acquirer must retain, and what would it take?                                                   |                   |

## Domain 6: Culture & Organizational Health

*The domain most commonly underweighted in financial due diligence and most commonly cited as the source of post-close problems. Two organizations can be financially compatible and culturally incompatible.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                                    |Response / Evidence|
|----|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|6.1 |How would leadership describe the target’s culture in three words? How would employees describe it? (If different, explore the gap.)  |                   |
|6.2 |What are the target’s stated values? Are they documented? How are they practiced — and how are violations handled?                    |                   |
|6.3 |What is the decision-making style? (Consensus-driven, top-down, data-driven, relationship-driven)                                     |                   |
|6.4 |Has the target conducted an engagement survey in the past 24 months? What were the key findings? What actions were taken?             |                   |
|6.5 |What is the management style of the CEO/founder? How dependent is the culture on that individual?                                     |                   |
|6.6 |How are internal communications managed? How do employees learn what’s happening in the organization?                                 |                   |
|6.7 |Are there employee resource groups, union representation, or works councils?                                                          |                   |
|6.8 |How does the target recognize and reward performance? Are there known inequities in recognition or advancement?                       |                   |
|6.9 |What is the target’s reputation as an employer in its market? (Glassdoor ratings, exit interview themes, recruiter feedback)          |                   |
|6.10|What are the top three concerns that target employees have about this transaction? (Ask leadership; then find a way to ask employees.)|                   |
|6.11|Has the target experienced any significant cultural disruptions — leadership changes, layoffs, reorgs — in the past two years?        |                   |

*Identify legal, financial, regulatory, and reputational risks that could affect deal value or create post-close liability.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                                    |Response / Evidence|
|----|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|7.1 |Are there any pending or threatened lawsuits, arbitrations, or regulatory proceedings?                                                |                   |

## Domain 8: Integration Planning

*Even a clean acquisition fails if integration is not planned. This domain assesses whether both organizations are prepared for the operational reality of combining.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                      |Response / Evidence|
|----|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|8.1 |What is the acquirer’s intended operating model for the target post-close? (Standalone, integrated, partial integration)|                   |
|8.2 |What is the plan for the target’s CEO and executive team? Retention, transition, or replacement?                        |                   |
|8.3 |What is the integration timeline? What milestones define progress? Who owns integration?                                |                   |
|8.4 |How will financial systems be consolidated? ERP, chart of accounts, fiscal year, audit firm?                            |                   |
|8.5 |How will HR systems and policies be harmonized? Which policies prevail during transition?                               |                   |
|8.6 |How will compensation and benefits be aligned? What is the impact on total comp cost?                                   |                   |
|8.7 |What is the communication plan for employees, customers, vendors, and other stakeholders?                               |                   |
|8.8 |What synergies are planned? What is the timeline for realizing them? What is the headcount impact?                      |                   |
|8.9 |What is the estimated cost of integration? (Systems, HR, legal, real estate, rebranding, severance)                     |                   |
|8.10|What are the conditions under which either party can walk away before close? What happens to exclusivity?               |                   |
|8.11|What regulatory approvals — antitrust, industry-specific, foreign investment — are required, and what is the timeline?  |                   |

## Common Post-Close Surprises (and How to Find Them Pre-Close)

|Surprise                                                                                                                                  |Where It Hides                                  |Domain to Probe|
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|---------------|
|**Compensation compression** — target pays above-market at junior levels, creating equity issues when integrated                          |Job level and salary range detail               |4              |
|**Unfunded pension liability** — defined benefit plan with material underfunding                                                          |Benefits detail, actuarial reports              |4              |
|**Contractor misclassification** — significant workforce labeled as contractors who meet employee tests                                   |Contractor arrangements, classification analysis|4              |
|**Change-of-control retention cost** — unvested equity or retention bonuses that accelerate and must be funded at close                   |Employment agreements, equity plan              |3, 4           |
|**Key person departure** — founder or technical leader who leaves within 60 days of close                                                 |Retention risk assessment, culture              |4, 6           |
|**Culture incompatibility** — acquirer’s process-driven culture collides with target’s entrepreneurial culture                            |Culture assessment, Glassdoor, exit themes      |6              |
|**Regulatory gap** — target operates in a regulated space with undisclosed compliance gaps                                                |Compliance posture, audit history               |7              |
|**Customer concentration** — 40% of revenue from one customer who is change-of-control sensitive                                          |Revenue breakdown, contract review              |1, 3           |
|**Integration cost underestimate** — systems integration, real estate rationalization, and HR harmonization cost 2–3× the initial estimate|Integration planning, systems inventory         |5, 8           |
|**Undisclosed litigation** — active or threatened claims that weren’t disclosed in initial data room                                      |Legal proceedings, whistleblower history        |7              |

## Relationship to Nonprofit Merger & Affiliation Readiness Assessment

This framework and the Nonprofit Merger & Affiliation Readiness Assessment share the same eight-domain architecture and the same underlying methodology. The differences are scope and language:

|Dimension             |Nonprofit Version                            |Enterprise Version                                                                                                          |
|----------------------|---------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Domain 1**          |Mission & Strategic Alignment                |Strategic Fit & Business Rationale                                                                                          |
|**Domain 3**          |Financial Health                             |Financial Position & Liabilities                                                                                            |
|**Domain 4**          |People & Employment                          |People & Employment (expanded: 16 vs. 14 questions, adds compensation compression, equity acceleration, classification risk)|
|**Domain 7**          |Risk & Compliance                            |Risk & Compliance (expanded: adds export controls, OFAC, sector-specific regulators)                                        |
|**Deal determination**|Proceed / Conditions / Defer / Do Not Proceed|Proceed / Conditions / Restructure / Do Not Proceed                                                                         |
|**Audience**          |Board committee or ED                        |HR leader, deal team, external advisor                                                                                      |

Either framework can be adapted for the other context. The nonprofit version can be tightened for small business acquisitions. The enterprise version can be simplified for nonprofit transactions of scale.

*In M&A, what you don’t know before the deal closes is what hurts you after. The organizations that get integration right are the ones that asked the uncomfortable questions before signing — and built the answers into the deal structure.*

*Due diligence is not suspicion. It is respect — for the people whose livelihoods depend on the transaction being done well.*
