# Governance Audit Reference Guide & Self-Assessment

## Accreditation Methodology Adapted for Board Self-Evaluation

*Governance Toolkit · Reference Series*

*Richard Porter · 2026*

*This is a self-assessment instrument adapted from healthcare accreditation methodology (JCAHO/Joint Commission). It allows any organization’s board to evaluate its own governance health using evidence-based standards — nonprofit, association, cooperative, or community entity. It is not an external accreditation and confers no certification. Its value is diagnostic: identifying what works, what doesn’t, and where to focus improvement efforts.*

-----

## Purpose

Healthcare organizations undergo accreditation because lives depend on governance quality. Organizations serving vulnerable populations, managing donated or public resources, and operating under community trust face stakes that are different in degree but not in kind. Yet most small organizations have no systematic way to evaluate whether their governance is functioning.

This tool adapts the accreditation methodology — standards, self-assessment, evidence collection, findings, corrective action — into a self-administered governance audit. The goal is not to pass or fail. The goal is to see clearly.

-----

## How the Audit Works

|#|Phase                    |JCAHO Equivalent                                             |Organization Version                                                          |
|-|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1|**Standards Review**     |Review applicable standards before self-assessment           |Board reviews the seven governance domains and their standards                |
|2|**Self-Assessment**      |Organization rates itself against each standard              |Board rates each standard as Met, Partially Met, or Not Met with evidence     |
|3|**Evidence Collection**  |Documentation gathered to support ratings                    |For each standard: what document, practice, or data point supports the rating?|
|4|**Findings & Priorities**|Survey team identifies deficiencies and required improvements|Board identifies its top 3 governance gaps and ranks them by urgency          |
|5|**Corrective Action**    |Organization submits improvement plan with deadlines         |Board assigns an owner, a deadline, and a success measure for each gap        |

**Frequency:** Annually. Conduct during Q4 so that corrective actions can be incorporated into the next year’s plan. A full audit takes approximately 90 minutes of board time if pre-work is distributed in advance.

**Who participates:** All board members rate independently. Results are aggregated and discussed as a group. The Board Chair or Governance Committee (if one exists) facilitates. The executive may provide evidence but does not rate governance standards — this is the board evaluating itself.

-----

## The Seven Governance Domains

The audit covers seven domains. Each domain contains three to five standards. Each standard is rated Met, Partially Met, or Not Met. Evidence is required for any rating of Met.

### Domain 1: Mission & Strategic Direction

*Does the board ensure the organization has a clear mission and is making measurable progress toward it?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                        |Rating                         |
|---|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|1.1|The organization has a written mission statement that is current, specific, and reviewed by the board within the past two years.|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|1.2|The board has defined measurable outcomes (Ends) that the organization exists to produce, distinct from operational activities. |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|1.3|The board reviews progress toward strategic goals or Ends at least quarterly, using data rather than anecdote.                  |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|1.4|The organization has a current strategic plan or documented strategic priorities with a defined time horizon.                   |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Mission statement with review date, Ends statement or strategic plan document, board meeting minutes showing quarterly outcome reviews, dashboard or report with outcome data.

-----

### Domain 2: Board Composition & Development

*Does the board have the right people, and does it invest in their effectiveness?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                                     |Rating                         |
|---|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|2.1|The board has a documented process for identifying, recruiting, and onboarding new members.                                                  |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|2.2|Board composition reflects the diversity of the community served, or there is a documented plan to address gaps.                             |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|2.3|All board members receive orientation within 60 days of joining that covers mission, bylaws, financial position, and governance expectations.|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|2.4|The board conducts or participates in governance training or education at least once per year.                                               |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|2.5|Term limits and succession planning are documented and followed.                                                                             |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Recruitment process documentation, board roster with demographic data, onboarding checklist, training attendance records, bylaws with term limit provisions, succession plan.

-----

### Domain 3: Financial Oversight

*Does the board ensure responsible stewardship of the organization’s financial resources?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                                 |Rating                         |
|---|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|3.1|The board reviews financial statements at least quarterly, including comparison to budget and prior year.                                |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|3.2|The organization undergoes an annual independent financial review or audit appropriate to its size.                                      |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|3.3|The board approves the annual budget before the fiscal year begins.                                                                      |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|3.4|Financial policies exist covering expenditure authority, reserves, and conflict of interest.                                             |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|3.5|The organization maintains adequate operating reserves (minimum: 3 months of operating expenses, or a board-approved alternative target).|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Board meeting minutes showing financial review, audit report or review letter, approved budget with date, financial policies document, reserve balance documentation.

-----

### Domain 4: Board–Executive Relationship

*Is the relationship between the board and the executive clearly defined, healthy, and productive?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                          |Rating                         |
|---|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|4.1|The executive has a current, written job description that clearly defines authority, responsibilities, and reporting relationship.|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|4.2|The board conducts a formal executive performance evaluation at least annually, based on documented criteria.                     |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|4.3|The boundary between board governance and executive operations is documented and understood by both parties.                      |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|4.4|The board has a documented executive succession or emergency leadership plan.                                                     |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Executive job description with date, evaluation form or summary, delegation policy or Ends/Means boundary document, succession plan.

-----

### Domain 5: Legal & Regulatory Compliance

*Does the organization meet its legal obligations and maintain its public trust?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                         |Rating                         |
|---|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|5.1|Bylaws are current, legally compliant, and have been reviewed within the past three years.                       |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|5.2|Required state and federal filings (IRS Form 990, state registration, etc.) are completed on time.               |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|5.3|The organization has appropriate insurance coverage (general liability, D&O, workers’ comp if applicable).       |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|5.4|A conflict of interest policy exists, is signed annually by all board members, and includes a disclosure process.|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Bylaws with review date, filed 990 with confirmation, insurance certificates, signed conflict of interest forms.

-----

### Domain 6: Board Operations & Meeting Effectiveness

*Does the board use its limited time effectively and maintain its own discipline?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                                                        |Rating                         |
|---|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|6.1|The board meets regularly (at least quarterly) with a quorum present and minutes recorded.                                                                      |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|6.2|Board meeting agendas are distributed in advance and allocate the majority of time to governance (strategy, monitoring, policy) rather than operational reports.|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|6.3|Board members regularly attend meetings (75%+ attendance rate across all members).                                                                              |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|6.4|The board evaluates its own performance annually using documented criteria.                                                                                     |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Meeting calendar and attendance records, sample agendas showing time allocation, recorded minutes, self-evaluation instrument or results.

-----

### Domain 7: Community Engagement & Accountability

*Is the organization transparent, accountable, and connected to the community it serves?*

|#  |Standard                                                                                                                             |Rating                         |
|---|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
|7.1|The organization communicates its mission, programs, and impact to the public at least annually (annual report, website, newsletter).|[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|7.2|The organization has a mechanism for receiving and responding to feedback from clients, volunteers, and community members.           |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|
|7.3|The organization’s financial information is publicly accessible (990 on file, website, GuideStar/Candid profile or equivalent).      |[ ] Met [ ] Partial [ ] Not Met|

**Evidence examples:** Annual report or equivalent publication, feedback mechanism documentation, public financial access documentation, website with mission and financial summary.

## Scoring & Interpretation

After all board members rate independently, aggregate the results. For each standard, determine the consensus rating:

- **Met:** Evidence exists, the practice is consistent, and the board agrees this is functioning well.
- **Partially Met:** Some evidence exists or the practice is inconsistent. Improvement is needed but the foundation is in place.
- **Not Met:** No evidence exists, the practice does not occur, or the board identifies this as a significant gap.

|Result          |Interpretation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**27–30 Met**   |Strong governance health. Focus on sustaining current practices and refining areas rated Partially Met. This board is functioning at a high level.                                                                                                  |
|**20–26 Met**   |Functional governance with identifiable gaps. Prioritize the Not Met standards and develop corrective action plans. Most small organizations fall in this range.                                                                                    |
|**15–19 Met**   |Governance gaps are significant and likely affecting organizational performance. Board should consider governance training, consultant engagement, or focused retreat time to address structural issues.                                            |
|**Below 15 Met**|Governance infrastructure needs fundamental development. This is not a criticism — many new or transitioning organizations start here. Prioritize: bylaws, financial oversight, and Board–Executive relationship clarity. Build from the foundation.|

## Corrective Action Plan

After the audit, the board selects its top three gaps (Not Met standards with the highest urgency or impact) and assigns corrective action:

|#|Gap (Standard #)                            |Owner |Deadline|Success Measure              |Status|
|-|--------------------------------------------|------|--------|-----------------------------|------|
|1|*[e.g., 4.4 — No executive succession plan]*|[Name]|[Date]  |[What does “done” look like?]|Open  |
|2|                                            |      |        |                             |      |
|3|                                            |      |        |                             |      |

**Rules for corrective action:**

- **Three maximum.** A board that tries to fix everything fixes nothing. Pick the three most urgent or impactful gaps. Address the rest in the next audit cycle.
- **Every action has an owner.** Not “the board” or “the Governance Committee.” A specific person who is accountable for completion.
- **Every action has a deadline.** Not “when we get to it.” A specific date, ideally before the next audit cycle.
- **Every action has a success measure.** Not “improve our financial oversight.” Rather: “A reserve policy is written, approved by the board, and reflected in the next budget.”
- **Status is reviewed quarterly.** Add corrective action status to the board’s quarterly agenda until all actions are complete or formally deferred with rationale.

## The Annual Governance Audit Cycle

|Timing                |Activity                                                                                                                                                      |
|----------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Q3 (Preparation)**  |Board Chair or Governance Committee distributes the self-assessment instrument to all board members. Members rate independently before the Q4 meeting.        |
|**Q4 (Audit Session)**|Dedicated board session (60–90 minutes) to review aggregated ratings, discuss disagreements, establish consensus ratings, and select top 3 corrective actions.|
|**Q1 (Action)**       |Corrective actions are underway. Owners report status at Q1 board meeting. New board members are oriented using audit results as governance context.          |
|**Q2 (Check)**        |Mid-year status review. Are corrective actions on track? Adjust deadlines or ownership if needed. Note emerging issues for next audit.                        |
|**Q3 (Repeat)**       |Distribute self-assessment for next annual cycle. Prior year’s corrective actions should be substantially complete.                                           |

## Common Pitfalls

|Pitfall                         |What It Looks Like                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
|--------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**The Generous Self-Assessment**|Board members rate everything as Met because they don’t want to criticize the organization or each other. Result: no gaps identified, no improvement made. Fix: require evidence for every Met rating. If you can’t point to a document, it’s not Met.|
|**The Audit That Doesn’t Close**|Gaps are identified but corrective actions are never assigned, tracked, or completed. The audit produces a list of problems that the board acknowledges and then ignores. Fix: the corrective action plan is the audit’s deliverable, not the ratings.|
|**The One-Person Audit**        |The Board Chair or executive completes the assessment alone and presents results to the board. This defeats the purpose: the value is in the discussion, not the scores. Fix: every board member rates independently; the board discusses as a group. |
|**The Compliance Trap**         |The board treats the audit as a checklist to pass rather than a diagnostic to learn from. Fix: focus the discussion on the Partially Met standards, where the most useful learning happens.                                                           |

## Toolkit Connections

- **Policy Governance (Carver):** Domain 1 (Mission/Ends) and Domain 4 (Board–Executive Relationship) map directly to Carver’s Ends and Board–Executive Linkage policies. If Carver is adopted, the audit standards can be updated to reflect Carver-specific language.
- **Change Management (Kotter):** Audit findings create natural urgency (Step 1) for governance improvement. The corrective action plan is the change initiative. Use the Kotter sequence to implement it.
- **Negative Space Mapper:** The Mapper identifies what’s missing. The audit evaluates what exists. Used together: Mapper first (what should we have?), audit second (do we have it, and is it working?).
- **People Pulse:** Engagement data feeds Domain 7 (Community Engagement). Departure data feeds Domain 2 (Board Composition) if board attrition is an issue.
- **Annual Board Report:** The audit’s corrective action plan and year-over-year comparison of ratings provides content for the governance section of the annual board report.

-----

*The board’s willingness to evaluate itself — honestly, with evidence, and with commitment to act on the findings — is the closest thing a small organization has to an accountability mechanism that doesn’t depend on crisis.*

*The audit that finds nothing wrong is the audit that wasn’t honest enough.*
