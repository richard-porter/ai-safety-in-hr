# Nonprofit Merger & Affiliation Readiness Assessment

## A Due Diligence Framework for Nonprofit Partnerships, Mergers, and Affiliations

*Governance Toolkit · Reference Series · Richard Porter · 2026*

*Adapted from enterprise HR due diligence methodology used in corporate acquisitions and integrations. Translated for nonprofit organizations considering mergers, affiliations, fiscal sponsorships, program absorptions, or other structural partnerships. This is a discovery instrument: it surfaces what you need to know before you commit, not after.*

-----

## Purpose

Nonprofit mergers, affiliations, and structural partnerships are increasing. Organizations share programs, consolidate back-office functions, absorb smaller nonprofits, or formally merge to survive funding contractions. Most of these partnerships are negotiated by board members and executive directors with deep mission expertise and limited experience in organizational integration.

The result is predictable: two organizations agree to combine without systematically understanding what they are combining. Financial statements are shared. Missions are compared. Bylaws are reviewed. But the operational infrastructure — people, policies, systems, contracts, compliance obligations, cultural norms — is discovered after the commitment is made, when surprises become problems.

This assessment adapts the enterprise due diligence process into a framework scaled for nonprofit partnerships. The enterprise version examines dozens of functional areas with teams of specialists. This version covers eight domains with questions that a board committee or ED can work through in structured conversations over two to four sessions.

**When to use this assessment:**

- **Merger:** Two organizations combining into one legal entity
- **Affiliation:** Formal partnership where organizations maintain separate identities but share governance, programs, or infrastructure
- **Program absorption:** One organization takes on another’s program, staff, and obligations
- **Fiscal sponsorship (incoming):** Your organization sponsors another entity and assumes fiduciary responsibility for their operations
- **Significant partnership:** Any agreement that involves shared staff, shared facilities, shared funding, or shared liability

**When NOT to use:** Routine collaborations, event co-sponsorships, referral agreements, or informal partnerships that do not involve shared governance, staff, or financial obligations.

-----

## How to Use This Assessment

The assessment covers eight domains. Each domain contains discovery questions organized from foundational (what exists?) to diagnostic (what could go wrong?). Work through each domain in structured conversations with the partner organization’s leadership. Document responses and evidence.

**Process:**

1. Assign a lead for each domain. In a small nonprofit, one or two people may cover all eight. In a larger organization, domain leads can be matched to expertise (e.g., treasurer leads Financial, board chair leads Governance).
1. Schedule discovery sessions with the partner organization. Plan two to four sessions of 90 minutes each, covering two to three domains per session.
1. Complete the discovery questions in each domain. Record responses, note evidence provided, and flag unanswered questions.
1. After all domains are complete, use the Risk Summary (final section) to compile findings and identify deal conditions, integration requirements, and potential deal-breakers.
1. Present findings to the full board before any binding commitment is made.

**Rating each domain** after completing discovery:

- **GREEN — Ready:** No significant gaps, risks, or unknowns. Integration can proceed with standard planning.
- **YELLOW — Conditional:** Manageable gaps or risks identified. Integration can proceed if specific conditions are met or mitigations are in place.
- **RED — Not Ready:** Significant gaps, risks, or unknowns that must be resolved before commitment. Potential deal-breaker if unresolvable.

## Domain 1: Mission & Strategic Alignment

*Before examining operational details, establish whether the partnership makes strategic sense. Misaligned missions produce dysfunctional organizations regardless of operational compatibility.*

|#  |Discovery Question                                                                                            |Response / Evidence|
|---|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|1.1|What is the partner organization’s mission statement? How does it align with or complement ours?              |                   |

## Domain 3: Financial Health

*Assess the partner’s financial position, sustainability, and any liabilities that would transfer. Financial due diligence is the domain most likely to reveal deal-breakers.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                            |Response / Evidence|
|----|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|3.1 |Provide audited or reviewed financial statements for the past three years.                                                    |                   |
|3.2 |What is the current operating reserve? How many months of expenses does it cover?                                             |                   |
|3.3 |Provide the current-year budget and year-to-date actual performance.                                                          |                   |
|3.4 |What are the partner’s revenue sources? Percentage breakdown by source (grants, donations, earned income, government, events).|                   |
|3.5 |Does any single funder represent more than 25% of total revenue? If so, what is the relationship status and renewal timeline? |                   |
|3.6 |Are there any outstanding debts, loans, lines of credit, or deferred obligations?                                             |                   |
|3.7 |Provide the most recent Form 990. Note any management letter comments from the auditor.                                       |                   |
|3.8 |Are there any pending grant deliverables, funder reports, or compliance deadlines that would transfer?                        |                   |
|3.9 |What payroll system is used? Who manages payroll? What is the pay cycle?                                                      |                   |
|3.10|Are there any deferred compensation arrangements, severance obligations, or retention agreements in effect?                   |                   |
|3.11|What is the partner’s annual audit cost? Who is the audit firm? Length of engagement?                                         |                   |

## Domain 4: People & Employment

*Understand the partner’s workforce — paid and volunteer — including compensation, benefits, policies, and any employment-related risks. In corporate acquisitions, this domain produces the most post-close surprises. The same is true in nonprofit partnerships.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                       |Response / Evidence|
|----|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|4.1 |How many paid staff? Full-time vs. part-time? Exempt vs. non-exempt breakdown?                                           |                   |

|4.3 |What is the compensation structure? Pay ranges, grade levels, or informal? When was it last reviewed?                    |                   |
|4.4 |What benefits are offered? (Health, dental, vision, retirement, PTO, other) Provide plan summaries and enrollment counts.|                   |
|4.5 |If retirement plan exists: what type (401k, 403b, pension), what is the match, what is the vesting schedule?             |                   |
|4.6 |What is the current attrition rate? Voluntary vs. involuntary? Any patterns or concerns?                                 |                   |
|4.7 |Are there any pending or recent employee relations issues? (Complaints, grievances, EEOC charges, litigation)            |                   |

## Domain 6: Culture & Communication

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                               |Response / Evidence|

|6.2 |What are the partner’s stated values? Are they documented? How are they practiced?                               |                   |

## Domain 8: Integration Planning

*Even if all other domains are GREEN, integration itself can fail if not planned. This domain assesses whether both organizations are prepared for the operational reality of combining.*

|#   |Discovery Question                                                                                                                            |Response / Evidence|
|----|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|
|8.1 |What governance structure will the combined entity use? How will board seats be allocated?                                                    |                   |
|8.2 |Who will serve as ED of the combined entity? If both organizations have EDs, what is the plan for the role that is eliminated or restructured?|                   |
|8.3 |What is the timeline for integration? What milestones define progress?                                                                        |                   |
|8.4 |How will financial systems be consolidated? Same chart of accounts? Same fiscal year? Same audit firm?                                        |                   |
|8.5 |How will employee/volunteer policies be harmonized? Whose policies prevail during transition?                                                 |                   |

## Common Pitfalls in Nonprofit Partnerships

|Pitfall                       |What It Looks Like                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Mission Infatuation**       |Both organizations are so excited about mission alignment that they skip operational due diligence entirely. “We serve the same community!” is not a sufficient basis for combining.                                             |
|**The Handshake Deal**        |Board chairs or EDs agree on terms informally, then present the partnership to their respective boards as a done deal. The boards feel pressured to approve without adequate review.                                             |
|**The Undisclosed Liability** |A pending lawsuit, an unfunded pension obligation, a funder about to pull out, a key employee about to leave — discovered after the commitment is made. This is the primary reason financial and legal due diligence exists.     |
|**Culture Collision**         |Organization A runs on consensus and volunteerism. Organization B runs on hierarchy and professionalism. Neither is wrong, but combining them without acknowledging the difference produces confusion, resentment, and attrition.|
|**The Disappearing Partner**  |The smaller organization’s identity, staff, and volunteers are absorbed rather than integrated. The partnership that was pitched as “combining strengths” feels like a takeover.                                                 |
|**Integration Without a Plan**|The partnership is approved. Day one arrives. Nobody has decided whose policies apply, whose payroll system runs, whose name goes on the door, or who reports to whom.                                                           |
|**The Rescue Merger**         |A financially healthy organization agrees to absorb a financially distressed one “to save the mission.” The distressed organization’s problems transfer but don’t resolve. Both organizations are now distressed.                |

## Toolkit Connections

- **Change Management (Kotter):** A merger or affiliation IS a major change event. After the readiness assessment, use the Kotter 8-Step framework to plan and execute the integration. The assessment provides urgency data (Step 1). The integration plan provides the vision (Step 3).
- **Governance Audit:** Run the audit on both organizations before combining. Two organizations with complementary gaps can strengthen each other. Two organizations with the same gaps will compound them.
- **Policy Governance (Carver):** If either or both organizations use Carver or Carver-informed governance, the integration must address how governance models will be harmonized. Domain 2 and Domain 8 cover this.
- **People Pulse:** Deploy Pulse Check surveys in both organizations before and after the partnership to track engagement, anxiety, and cultural integration. Stay Interviews are particularly valuable during integration to identify retention risks.
- **Annual Board Report:** The first annual board report of the combined entity should include a dedicated section on integration progress.
- **Negative Space Mapper:** Run the Mapper on the partner organization during due diligence. What governance infrastructure is missing? The answer tells you what you’ll need to build post-partnership.

-----

*In corporate acquisitions, due diligence teams spend months examining every aspect of the target company before committing. They do this not because they are cautious by nature but because they learned — through expensive failures — that what you don’t know before the deal closes is what hurts you after. Nonprofits make the same mistakes with less margin for error.*

*Due diligence is not suspicion. It is respect — for the mission, for the people, and for the communities that depend on both organizations getting this right.*
