## The Problem This Role Solves

ERISA fiduciary duty does not change because AI is administering the plan. The standard — prudent expert, acting solely in participants’ interest — applies regardless of whether a human or an algorithm made the eligibility determination.

What changes is the nature of the oversight obligation. A fiduciary who delegates benefits administration to an AI tool has not delegated the fiduciary duty. They have accepted responsibility for a system making decisions at a volume no human could individually review, with failure modes that may be invisible until a participant is harmed.

> Benefits disputes are the first wave of AI-related employment litigation most organizations will actually face. The harm is concrete, the dollar amounts are specific, and statutory appeal rights exist under ERISA that do not require the employee to prove intent — only that the determination was wrong.

> **The Fiduciary Gap:** Most organizations deploying AI in benefits administration have not asked: who is exercising fiduciary oversight of this system? This role fills that gap.

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Fiduciary Oversight of AI-Assisted Administration

- Establish and maintain the oversight protocol: what decisions AI makes autonomously, what requires human review, what audit cadence applies
- Conduct periodic audits of AI-generated eligibility determinations against plan document requirements — on a defined schedule, not only when problems are suspected
- Document the oversight activity: what was reviewed, when, by whom, what was found
- Maintain the plan document as the governing standard — flag when AI behavior diverges from plan document intent

### 2. The Employee Dispute Path

- Design and maintain the ERISA-compliant appeal process for AI-generated benefits determinations
- Ensure the appeal path is genuinely accessible — not buried in a portal, not requiring re-litigation with the AI system
- When a determination is appealed, produce the documentation the review requires
- Partner with the AI Decision Audit Analyst to reconstruct AI decision chains for disputed determinations

### 3. Plan Integrity Monitoring

- Monitor AI-generated determinations for systematic errors: patterns affecting a class of participants, configuration errors producing consistent wrong outcomes, model behavior diverging from plan intent over time
- Distinguish between one-off errors and systematic failures — a pattern affecting the same demographic group is a fiduciary breach
- Maintain the error log and correction record
- Report systematically to plan fiduciaries on AI administration integrity on a defined cadence

### 4. Vendor Oversight

- Evaluate benefits administration AI vendors against fiduciary standards before deployment
- Maintain ongoing vendor oversight — when the tool is updated, assess whether fiduciary documentation requirements are still met
- Own the contract provisions governing AI vendor accountability

### 5. ADA and Accommodation Interface

- Track the emerging intersection of AI benefits administration and accommodation obligations
- Monitor regulatory and legal developments on AI in benefits administration
- Partner with the AI Workforce Wellness Lead on benefits design addressing AI-related occupational health patterns

## The Four Disputes This Role Will See First

|Dispute Type                                       |What It Is                                                                           |What This Role Does                                                                                                                                                                            |
|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|AI-generated eligibility denial                    |Employee submits claim; AI determines ineligible; employee disagrees                 |Produce the determination record; if wrong, correct and log; if right, explain in plain language                                                                                               |
|Systematic exclusion pattern                       |Multiple employees in same demographic group denied same benefit through same AI path|Potential fiduciary breach — escalate immediately to legal and plan fiduciaries                                                                                                                |
|AI determination the plan document does not support|AI diverges from plan document through configuration error or model drift            |Correct the AI output, not the plan document; document the divergence and correction                                                                                                           |
|Employee cannot understand why they were denied    |AI-generated denial language that does not explain the actual reason                 |ERISA requires specific reason in plain language — AI-generated language that doesn’t meet this standard is a compliance failure regardless of whether the underlying determination was correct|

## Qualifications

**Required:**

- Deep expertise in ERISA plan administration, fiduciary standards, and benefits compliance
- Direct experience managing ERISA appeals processes and benefits-related regulatory proceedings
- Working knowledge of AI tool behavior in administrative contexts: systematic error patterns, configuration drift, audit documentation requirements
- Ability to translate plan document provisions into plain language for employees and AI system behavior into terms a fiduciary review can assess
- CEBS or equivalent

**Preferred:**

- Experience in DOL audit response or ERISA litigation support
- Background in benefits plan design — understanding plan intent as well as plan language
- Exposure to benefits administration technology implementation

**Not Required:**

- A legal degree — this role works alongside ERISA counsel
- Deep expertise in benefits administration execution — that function is substantially automated
