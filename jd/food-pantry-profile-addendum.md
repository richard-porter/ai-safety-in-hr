## Completed Role Profile: Food Pantry Manager

Adapted from the Generic Role Profile Template (Mr. Wolff v2.0). This is the first sector-specific completion of that template. Use it to assess an incumbent, evaluate a candidate, or define the role for a pantry transitioning from informal to structured leadership.

-----

### Key Accountabilities

- Manage inventory flow: receiving, sorting, storage, and distribution of food (including perishables)
- Coordinate volunteer recruitment, scheduling, training, and retention
- Ensure regulatory and food safety compliance (health department, food bank agreements, temperature logging)
- Maintain client-facing service that preserves dignity, choice, and privacy
- Manage donor and food bank relationships without ceding operational control
- Track and report operational data: clients served, pounds distributed, waste, volunteer hours
- Execute or coordinate compliance filings, insurance, and documentation
- Communicate operational needs and constraints to the board without making governance decisions

-----

### Key Leadership Competencies

- **Priority Setting:** Daily triage across competing demands with limited resources (Scale Test, FP-1)
- **Composure:** Steady decision-making under operational pressure, especially during surge periods
- **Directing Others:** Coordinating volunteer labor with varying skill levels, availability, and motivation
- **Ethics and Values:** Maintaining equitable distribution practices and confronting drift when rules create barriers (FP-8)
- **Organizational Agility:** Navigating board, donors, food bank, regulators, and community expectations simultaneously

-----

### Key Technical/Professional Competencies

- **Inventory Management:** Perishable triage, FIFO rotation, waste tracking, storage capacity planning (FP-1)
- **Food Safety & Regulatory Compliance:** Health department requirements, temperature logging, safe handling protocols
- **Volunteer Coordination:** Scheduling, cross-training, burnout monitoring, role documentation (FP-3, FP-7)
- **Supply Chain Management:** Source diversification, food bank ordering, donor relationship maintenance (FP-5)
- **Data Tracking & Reporting:** Client counts, distribution volumes, waste logs, compliance records
- **Client Service Delivery:** Choice-based distribution, privacy protection, language access, dignity preservation (FP-2)

-----

### Key Experiences

- Direct food handling or food service operations (commercial, institutional, or pantry)
- Volunteer management or coordination in any setting
- Working with vulnerable or underserved populations
- Basic compliance or regulatory experience (any sector)
- Operating under resource constraints with competing priorities

-----

### Key Relationships

**Internal:** Board of Directors, volunteers, other staff (if any)

**External — Sourcing:** Regional food bank, grocery/retail donors, community food drive organizers, farm partners

**External — Oversight:** Health department, state charitable registration, insurance carriers

**External — Community:** Clients/families, referring organizations, partner nonprofits, faith communities

### Using the Skills Gap Results

**Score of 1 (Underdeveloped):** Immediate training priority. Pair with an experienced volunteer or mentor. Run the linked tool as a guided exercise, not solo.

**Score of 2 (Adequate):** Monitor and develop. Identify one specific growth action per quarter. The linked tool is the practice ground.

**Score of 3 (Very Competent):** This person can teach others. Use them to cross-train backups (FP-3, FP-7).

**Priority logic:** Address skills scoring 1 in categories that affect client safety or regulatory compliance first (Food Safety, Client Service). Then address operational sustainability (Volunteer Coordination, Supply Chain). Then planning and communication.

## Volunteer Talent Calibration

Connects the Talent Calibration Grid (Mr. Wolff v2.0) to FP-3 (Volunteer Fragility Map) and FP-7 (Key-Person Dependency Detector). Those tools assess what breaks when someone leaves. This section assesses capability and potential while they’re still here.

|                    |Low Performance                                                                                             |Medium Performance                                                                                                       |High Performance                                                                                                      |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**High Potential**  |**New Volunteers** — Enthusiastic, untested. Need structured onboarding and early wins. Don’t overload.     |**Emerging Leaders** — Consistent, developing. Ready for cross-training into backup roles (FP-3 targets).                |**Future Coordinators** — Proven, capable. First candidates for role expansion, shift leadership, or paid conversion. |
|**Medium Potential**|**Mismatches** — Willing but struggling. May need a different role within the pantry, not necessarily exit. |**Reliable Core** — Solid, dependable. Essential to operations. Recognize and retain. These are your valued contributors.|**Specialists** — High performance in specific area. Developing breadth. Cross-train to reduce fragility (FP-7).      |
|**Low Potential**   |**At Risk** — Not meeting needs, limited growth trajectory. Honest conversation needed. May self-select out.|**Question Marks** — Adequate but high-maintenance. Requires disproportionate support. Monitor cost-to-benefit.          |**Anchors** — High performance, narrow scope. Critical to retain in current role. Single-point-of-failure risk (FP-7).|

### How to Use This with FP-3 and FP-7

**FP-3 (Volunteer Fragility Map)** identifies single points of failure — roles with one person and no trained backup. The calibration grid tells you **who** can become the backup:

- Emerging Leaders and Future Coordinators are your first cross-training candidates
- Reliable Core volunteers are your retention priorities — don’t burn them out filling gaps
- Anchors are your highest fragility risk — high performance, but if they leave, a function stops

**FP-7 (Key-Person Dependency Detector)** maps which functions live inside one person’s head. The calibration grid tells you **whether** a transfer is feasible:

- If the key person is an **Anchor** (high performance, low potential for growth): document everything they do. They won’t expand, so the knowledge must be externalized.
- If the key person is a **Future Coordinator**: they’re ready to delegate. The problem is willingness, not capability.
- If the key person is **the pantry manager**: that’s the real finding. Use FP-7’s honest check.

**Frequency:** Run the calibration annually, or when the volunteer roster changes significantly. The FP-3 fragility map is monthly; the calibration grid provides the strategic layer behind it.

## Intervention Planning for Food Pantry Roles

Step 4 of the 4-Step Model applied to food pantry context. For each gap identified in the Skills Gap Analysis, select the appropriate intervention type.

|Intervention Type       |Description                                                                                             |Food Pantry Examples                                                                                                                                                     |
|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Shadowing**           |Observe a competent performer during live operations. No responsibility, only observation and questions.|New volunteer shadows distribution lead for two sessions before taking a role. New manager shadows food bank ordering process.                                           |
|**Guided Practice**     |Perform the task with a competent performer present and coaching in real time.                          |First solo perishable triage with experienced volunteer reviewing decisions. First health department log entry with manager checking.                                    |
|**Tool-Based Learning** |Run the linked Sovereign Tool as a structured exercise, not just as an operational task.                |Walk through FP-2 (Client Dignity Audit) together as a training exercise before the manager runs it solo. Run FP-5 (Supply Chain Concentration Test) as a board workshop.|
|**Documentation Review**|Study existing written procedures. Only effective if written procedures actually exist.                 |Review compliance calendar (Compliance Canary output). Review food bank agreement requirements. Review distribution policies before running FP-8.                        |
|**External Training**   |Formal training from food bank, health department, or nonprofit association. Usually free.              |Food bank’s volunteer orientation. Health department food safety certification. State nonprofit association’s board governance training.                                 |
|**Peer Exchange**       |Visit or consult with another pantry manager facing similar challenges. Not mentoring — mutual learning.|Visit a pantry that runs choice-based distribution. Talk to a manager who successfully transitioned from key-person dependency. Connect through food bank network.       |

**Intervention selection logic:** Match the intervention to the score. Score of 1 starts with Shadowing or External Training. Score of 2 benefits from Guided Practice or Tool-Based Learning. Score of 3 means this person delivers the intervention to others.

## How to Use This Addendum

**For a new pantry manager hire or appointment:** Complete the Skills Gap Analysis. Identify scores of 1. Build an intervention plan for the first 90 days focused on client safety and regulatory compliance skills.

**For an incumbent pantry manager:** Self-assess using the Skills Gap Analysis annually. Compare against the Role Profile. Identify one development priority per quarter.

**For volunteer assessment:** Run the Talent Calibration Grid annually. Cross-reference with FP-3 (Fragility Map) monthly output. Ensure Anchors are documented and Emerging Leaders are being developed.

**For board oversight:** The completed Role Profile defines what “pantry manager” means. Use it for annual evaluation, compensation review (if paid), or succession planning. The board evaluates against this profile, not against informal expectations.
