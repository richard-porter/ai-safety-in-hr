> **Role in the Ecosystem:** This is the junior practitioner role that the senior governance function depends on. When a dispute arises, when a proceeding begins, when an auditor asks what happened — the AI Decision Audit Analyst does the forensic reconstruction. They do not make the determination. They produce the record that makes a defensible determination possible.

-----

## The Problem This Role Solves

Every AI-related HR dispute, investigation, and regulatory proceeding requires the same foundational work before anything else can happen: reconstruct what the AI actually did.

- Which model version was running at the time of the decision?
- What data was provided as input?
- What was the output — exactly, before any human modification?
- What did the human reviewer do with that output, and when?
- Is the documentation complete, or are there gaps?

This work is specific, technical, and time-consuming. Senior practitioners cannot do it without abandoning everything else. And leaving it undone means a dispute record with gaps — which is a liability.

> *“The document that does not exist is as significant as the document that does. Your job is to find both.”*

-----

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Decision Chain Reconstruction

- For any AI-assisted HR decision under review: reconstruct the complete chain — model version, input data, output produced, human review step, final decision taken
- Identify documentation gaps explicitly — absence is data
- Produce a standardized Decision Audit Report for each matter reviewed
- Maintain version records: what AI tools were running at what times, what configuration changes occurred

### 2. Dispute Support

- Provide the factual reconstruction that the Dispute Resolution Lead needs to run a proceeding
- Support the Benefits Fiduciary Lead in reconstructing AI-generated benefits determinations for ERISA appeals
- Support the Pay Equity Lead in reconstructing compensation AI outputs for regulatory responses
- Prepare technical summaries accessible to employees, arbitrators, and regulators

### 3. Documentation Audit Cadence

- Run periodic audits of AI tool documentation completeness on a defined schedule — not only when a dispute arises
- Identify documentation gaps before they become dispute vulnerabilities
- Maintain the AI tool inventory for HR: what tools are in use, what decisions they touch, what documentation each produces

### 4. The Translation Function

- Produce plain-language accounts of technical findings that non-specialists can rely on
- Answer the questions a dispute process actually asks: what happened, in what order, is the record complete?
- Prepare briefing materials for senior practitioners before proceedings

## What This Role Is Not

|Not                  |Why the Distinction Matters                                                                               |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|A decision-maker     |The Analyst reconstructs the record; the determination belongs to the senior roles they support           |
|A technical engineer |Reads and interprets AI system documentation; does not build, configure, or modify AI systems             |
|A legal function     |Produces the factual record that legal counsel and ER practitioners work with                             |
|A compliance function|Supports compliance by maintaining documentation standards; the Chain of Custody Lead owns the obligations|

## Qualifications

**Required:**

- 2–5 years of experience in HR operations, HRIS administration, compliance analysis, internal audit, or a related field with significant documentation and record management components
- Demonstrated ability to read and interpret technical records and translate findings into plain language
- Strong written communication: structured, clear, precise
- Comfort with incomplete information — the record is frequently incomplete, and the analyst must document what is missing as clearly as what is present

**Preferred:**

- Experience with HR technology systems (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors, ATS platforms) at an administrative or configuration level
- Background in internal audit, compliance, or legal document review
- Coursework or certification in AI fundamentals
- Exposure to EEOC charge response, ERISA appeals, or employment litigation support in an administrative capacity

**Not Required:**

- A computer science background or AI engineering expertise
- Senior HR experience
- Legal training
