## The Problem This Role Solves

When an AI-assisted employment decision is challenged — in court, in a DOL audit, in an EEOC investigation — someone in your organization will be asked:

- Which AI tool produced this output?
- Which version of that tool was running at the time?
- Which human reviewed the output before it influenced the personnel record?
- What was the date and documented basis of that human decision?
- Was the employee informed that AI was involved?

Right now, in most organizations, nobody can answer these questions. Not legal. Not IT. Not HR leadership. The AI vendor explicitly does not own it.

This role owns it.

> *“The AI vendor is not liable for how their tool is used in your employment decisions. You are. This role exists so that ‘you’ is a person with a name, a process, and a document.”*

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Chain of Custody Documentation System

- Design and maintain the documentation architecture recording AI involvement in every personnel decision
- Enforce the standard: model version, prompt or configuration, output, timestamp, reviewing human identity, decision taken
- Conduct quarterly audits of chain-of-custody compliance across all AI-enabled HR workflows
- Build the escalation protocol for decisions where chain of custody is broken or incomplete

### 2. The Wrongful Termination Test — Operational Enforcement

- Apply the Wrongful Termination Test to every AI tool before deployment
- Maintain a live register of AI tools in HR use, each mapped to its Ulrich quadrant and risk level
- Conduct post-incident reviews whenever an AI-assisted decision results in a grievance, EEOC charge, or legal action
- Serve as the HR subject matter expert in any litigation or regulatory proceeding involving AI-assisted decisions

### 3. AI Tool Governance — Ongoing Operations

- Own the AI governance calendar: model version tracking, audit cycles, vendor review schedules
- Review and approve all AI tool deployments in HR before go-live — with authority to delay pending chain-of-custody infrastructure
- Monitor AI tool behavior post-deployment for known failure modes: demographic drift, output homogenization, false precision, proxy variable creep
- Partner with Legal on discovery response preparation for AI-related employment claims

### 4. Human Review Gate Design

- Design the human review step between every AI output and every personnel action — not a checkbox, a documented decision with a named human owner
- Train HR professionals on what meaningful AI output review looks like
- Build the appeal path that allows an employee to challenge any decision in which AI was involved
- Ensure employees are informed when AI is involved in a process that affects them

### 5. Quadrant-Specific Risk Management

- Maintain the quadrant risk register
- Apply heightened oversight to Employee Champion quadrant deployments
- Enforce hard constraints in Employee Champion contexts: no autonomous case decisions, explicit AI disclosure, guaranteed human escalation within defined SLA
- Flag and escalate any deployment that crosses quadrant boundaries without updated risk assessment

## The Qualification That Matters Most

> The practitioner who fills this role has been in a room — a deposition, a DOL audit, an EEOC investigation, an unemployment hearing — and has had to produce documentation under adversarial conditions. They know the feeling of the document that exists versus the document that should have existed. That experience is not replaceable by legal training, compliance certification, or AI literacy. It is the prerequisite everything else builds on.

-----

## Qualifications

**Required:**

- 10+ years of HR experience with direct exposure to employee relations, HR compliance, or employment law administration
- Personal experience in at least one of: EEOC charge response, DOL audit, unemployment hearing, wrongful termination litigation, or formal internal investigation
- Working knowledge of AI system behavior — specifically: proxy variable risk, output homogenization, false precision, demographic drift
- Demonstrated ability to build and maintain documentation systems under operational pressure
- Comfort operating in contested, adversarial environments without losing procedural discipline
- SPHR, SHRM-SCP, or equivalent

**Preferred:**

- Experience with AI tool procurement evaluation or deployment governance
- Background in Six Sigma, DMAIC, or process audit methodology
- Exposure to HR technology implementation
- Experience in shared services or COE environment

**Not Required:**

- A legal degree — this role works alongside employment counsel
- A computer science background

## Guiding Principles

- **Accountability is not a policy.** It is a named person who owns a decision and can defend it.
- **Chain of custody is not optional.** Every AI-touched personnel decision must be traceable.
- **The Employee Champion quadrant has hard constraints.** These do not dissolve under velocity pressure.
- **Absence is data.** The document that does not exist is as significant as the document that does.
- **This role does not obstruct deployment.** It makes deployment defensible.
