## The Problem This Role Solves

Every organization deploying AI has institutional incentives to communicate optimistically about what AI does. Every vendor selling AI tools wants your employees to trust them. Every efficiency-focused executive wants adoption metrics to go up.

Nobody in that ecosystem has a strong institutional incentive to tell your workforce the honest version of what is happening to them.

What AI tools do to human cognition, relationships, and work patterns over time is documented, specific, and largely absent from organizational AI training programs. This role is the independent voice that provides it.

-----

## Two Functions, One Role

|AI Workforce Education                                                                                         |AI Workforce Wellness                                                                                               |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Teaches employees what AI tools actually do — including failure modes, cognitive effects, and patterns to avoid|Identifies, names, and responds to AI-related occupational health patterns as they emerge                           |
|Produces plain-language research summaries                                                                     |Builds monitoring and intervention infrastructure for AI-related workforce maladies                                 |
|The voice that tells employees what is happening to their work before they figure it out the hard way          |The function that catches what is happening to employees before it becomes a crisis                                 |
|Closest analogy: the public health communicator who explains risk without inducing panic                       |Closest analogy: the occupational health practitioner who documented repetitive strain before it had a clinical name|

*At ~5,000 employees, the Education function separates first. At ~10,000 employees or after the first significant AI-related incident, the Wellness function separates. Both retain editorial independence from AI adoption teams and vendor relationships.*

## The AI-Related Workforce Maladies This Role Addresses

|Malady                                |Mechanism                                                                                                    |Workforce Signal                                                                                                                  |Affected Population                                           |
|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Cognitive Delegation Syndrome**     |Gradual outsourcing of analytical and compositional work to AI, atrophying capacity for independent reasoning|Inability to produce first drafts; dependence on AI to initiate complex tasks; discomfort with ambiguous problems                 |Knowledge workers and analysts                                |
|**Sycophantic Drift (Org Expression)**|AI-generated consensus persistently smooths conflict and decision-making at team or organizational level     |Teams stop disagreeing; strategy outputs represent nobody’s judgment; leaders lose ability to read the room                       |Teams and leadership in AI-mediated communication environments|
|**Parasocial Dependency (Workplace)** |AI tools substitute for the human relationships healthy work requires                                        |Employees feel more understood by AI than colleagues; AI intermediaries replace direct communication                              |Individuals in high-AI-use, low-human-contact environments    |
|**Atrophied Judgment — Management**   |Managers delegating people decisions to AI tools progressively lose relational and judgmental capacity       |AI-required initiation of any difficult conversation; generic performance documentation; employees report feedback feels automated|Management layer                                              |
|**Verification Paralysis**            |AI access makes decision-making slower — infinite plausible output creates inability to conclude             |More research, fewer decisions; increasing data access correlates with decreasing confidence                                      |Analytical roles with full AI access                          |


> **The Occupational Health Analogy:** The organization that documented repetitive strain injury patterns in the early 1980s — before OSHA requirements, before clinical names — was not being alarmist. It was being early. The AI-related workforce health patterns above are at the same stage RSI was in 1982.

## The Benefits COE Connection

- **EAP expansion:** AI-related cognitive and relational patterns will increasingly present in EAP utilization. This role works with EAP providers to ensure clinicians understand the specific patterns.
- **Accommodation implications:** As AI-related cognitive patterns become more documented, organizations will face accommodation requests. The ADA question — whether AI-induced cognitive impacts constitute conditions requiring accommodation — has not been litigated. This role tracks it before it becomes a crisis.
- **Benefits design:** Organizations that are honest about AI-related workforce health will eventually build benefits that address it. This role provides the intelligence that makes those benefits real rather than performative.

-----

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Workforce AI Education Program

- Design and deliver AI literacy education that is honest about failure modes, not just optimistic about capabilities
- Produce plain-language research summaries translating AI behavioral research into workforce-applicable guidance
- Develop role-specific curricula: what a people manager needs to understand differs from what a knowledge worker needs
- Build the vocabulary the organization uses to talk about AI risks — so employees can name what they are experiencing
- Update content continuously as AI tools evolve

### 2. AI Workforce Wellness Monitoring

- Design and maintain monitoring signals for AI-related workforce health patterns
- Partner with EAP providers to build referral pathways and clinician literacy for AI-specific presenting patterns
- Conduct periodic organizational health assessments focused on AI-related cognitive and relational patterns
- Build the early warning system: what signals indicate AI-related workforce health issues before they become acute
- Track emerging research on AI-related occupational health

### 3. The Honest Broker Function

- Provide the organization with an independent assessment of AI tool impacts on workforce health — independent of the vendor relationship and adoption pressure
- Flag patterns that adoption metrics will not surface
- Brief senior leadership with the same directness the public health communicator uses: here is what the research shows, here is the mechanism, here is what to watch for
- Serve as the internal voice that does not have an agenda on AI adoption

### 4. Benefits COE Interface

- Own AI-related EAP education and clinician preparation
- Track accommodation requests and potential legal developments
- Advise on benefits design that addresses AI-related workforce health honestly
- Partner with the Chain of Custody Lead on AI tool disclosures to employees

## The Qualification That Matters Most

> The practitioner who fills this role has done independent research on AI behavioral patterns and is not enthusiastic about what they found. They have read the studies. They understand the mechanisms. And they can communicate what they know to a room full of people who have varying levels of investment in not hearing it — without losing them, without alarming them past the point of action, and without softening the findings to the point of uselessness.

-----

## Qualifications

**Required:**

- Background in adult education, organizational development, workforce training, or occupational health
- Independent working knowledge of AI behavioral research — sycophantic drift, fabrication patterns, parasocial dependency mechanisms, documented real-world harm cases
- Ability to read and synthesize academic and clinical research and translate into plain-language organizational guidance
- Comfort delivering content that is unwelcome to some audiences without becoming adversarial
- Understanding of EAP systems, benefits administration, and workforce health infrastructure

**Preferred:**

- Clinical background in psychology, counseling, or occupational health
- Research literacy: ability to evaluate study methodology and distinguish strong findings from weak ones
- Experience in public health communication
- Familiarity with AI behavioral diagnostics in the Adult Mode Safety Ledger and Sovereign Thinking Tools frameworks
- Nonprofit or mission-driven organizational experience

**Not Required:**

- A computer science background
- Enthusiasm for AI
- A clinical license
