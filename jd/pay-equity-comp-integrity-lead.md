## The Problem This Role Solves

The Accountability Architect signs the pay equity audit. This role builds what is worth signing.

AI has compressed the execution layer of compensation work. Market pricing, band modeling, merit matrix generation — tools now do this faster and at greater scale than any human analyst. What has not been compressed is the design of the framework governing how those tools are used and how their outputs are evaluated for fairness and legal defensibility.

> **The Core Risk AI Creates in Compensation:** Compensation AI tools produce recommendations without visible methodology. Confidence intervals frequently do not exist. Proxy variable access — job title patterns, tenure distributions, geographic data — produces disparate impact that accumulates invisibly until an audit or litigation surfaces it.

## The Four AI Risks Specific to Compensation

|Risk                                        |Mechanism                                                                                                                                                 |Mitigation Design                                                                                    |
|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Proxy Variable Accumulation**             |Job title, tenure, location, and work history data — individually manageable, but in combination can reconstruct protected class status with high accuracy|Design testing that treats variable combinations, not just individual variables, as potential proxies|
|**False Precision in Band Recommendations** |AI tools produce specific numbers without confidence intervals; managers treat them as authoritative                                                      |Require uncertainty ranges as a procurement condition                                                |
|**Demographic Drift Between Cycles**        |A tool passing disparity testing in Year 1 may fail in Year 3 as workforce composition changes and model training data ages                               |Run disparity testing on a defined annual schedule, not only reactively                              |
|**Classification Errors Automated at Scale**|An AI tool misclassifying exempt/non-exempt status does so consistently across every affected role                                                        |Audit classification logic before automation                                                         |

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Pay Equity Framework Design

- Design and maintain the statistical methodology for pay equity analysis — regression frameworks, cohort construction, control variable selection, significance thresholds
- Determine which variables AI tools are permitted to access and which are prohibited
- Establish the demographic parity testing protocol that runs before any compensation cycle
- Build the internal equity architecture — job leveling logic, band structure, classification criteria

### 2. Compensation AI Oversight

- Evaluate compensation AI tools before deployment: does the tool produce uncertainty ranges? Does it expose methodology? Can disparity testing be run against its outputs?
- Maintain the testing cadence: run demographic parity analysis on AI outputs on a defined schedule
- Document proxy variable risk for every tool in the compensation stack
- Flag outputs showing disparity patterns to the Accountability Architect and legal before they influence decisions

### 3. The Pre-Decisional Audit

- Run a pre-decisional audit before each merit cycle, promotion cycle, and compensation structure review
- Produce a pre-decisional audit report the Accountability Architect can review and sign before the cycle runs — not after
- Build the process that makes “we tested this before the decision” the standard, not the exception

### 4. Methodology Documentation

- Maintain comprehensive documentation of every analytical methodology in use
- Document methodology changes over time with rationale
- Produce the methodology brief that legal can use in regulatory proceedings

### 5. Employee Dispute Support

- When an employee disputes a compensation decision, provide the analytical reconstruction
- Partner with the AI Dispute Resolution Lead on compensation disputes involving AI-assisted decisions
- Identify whether a dispute reveals a systemic pattern

## Qualifications

**Required:**

- Deep expertise in compensation analytics: regression methodology, cohort analysis, statistical significance, disparity testing frameworks
- Direct experience conducting pay equity analyses in a regulated environment — actual analyses with actual legal stakes
- Working knowledge of AI tool behavior specific to compensation: proxy variable risk, output confidence limitations, demographic drift patterns
- Ability to translate statistical methodology into plain language for legal, HR leadership, and regulatory audiences
- CEBS, CCP, or equivalent

**Preferred:**

- Experience in OFCCP audit response or EEOC compensation charge defense
- Background in statistical analysis or econometrics
- Exposure to HR technology implementation with focus on compensation systems
- Six Sigma or DMAIC methodology

**Not Required:**

- A legal degree
- Deep expertise in compensation administration — that function is handled by AI tools and shared services
