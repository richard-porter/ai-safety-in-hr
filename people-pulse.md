# People Pulse

## Engagement · Stay Interviews · Suggestion Loop · Departure Debrief

*A cause-agnostic system for knowing whether your people — volunteers and employees alike — are thriving, surviving, or leaving.*

*Operates under the Frozen Kernel. Never inside it.*

*Companion to: Mr. Wolff Rescue Kit · Retention Diagnostic (Tool 39) · Volunteer Fragility Map (FP-3)*

*Population = 1*

🧊

-----

## Why This Exists

Enterprise organizations spend millions on engagement surveys, stay interview programs, suggestion platforms, and exit interview processes. They’re separate systems run by separate teams with separate budgets.

Small organizations need all four functions and have zero of the infrastructure. Volunteers don’t fill out Gallup surveys. They just stop showing up. By the time you notice, they’re gone, and so is everything they knew.

This tool combines four enterprise engagement functions into one lightweight system designed for organizations with 5–50 people (any mix of paid staff and volunteers):

|Pulse Check               |Stay Interview                     |Suggestion Loop                          |Departure Debrief            |
|--------------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
|How are people feeling?   |Why are they still here?           |What ideas are being lost?               |Why did they leave?          |
|*Quarterly*               |*Annually per person*              |*Ongoing*                                |*Within 2 weeks of departure*|
|Detect drift before crisis|Prevent departure before it happens|Capture ideas with visible follow-through|Learn from loss              |

**The thread connecting all four:** people who feel heard stay longer, contribute more, and tell you what’s broken before it becomes a crisis. People who don’t feel heard just leave.

-----

## Volunteer vs. Employee: Same Tool, Different Tone

Every component of this tool works for both volunteers and paid staff, but the framing matters. Volunteers are not employees. They can’t be required to participate, can’t be “performance managed” in the traditional sense, and will leave the moment the experience stops being worth their time.

|                     |Employees                                                  |Volunteers                                                                        |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Pulse Check**      |Can be expected to complete. Schedule during work time.    |Must be invited, not required. Keep it under 3 minutes. Offer anonymity.          |
|**Stay Interview**   |Supervisor-led. Part of performance relationship.          |Peer-led or coordinator-led. Frame as gratitude, not assessment.                  |
|**Suggestion Loop**  |Formal channel. Response expected within defined timeframe.|Low-barrier channel (text, conversation, card). Response must be visible and warm.|
|**Departure Debrief**|Standard exit interview. Can be structured.                |Often there is no formal departure. Detect absence. Reach out. Listen.            |

## Component 1: Pulse Check

**What it replaces:** Annual engagement surveys (Gallup Q12, Glint, Culture Amp). Those are 40–80 questions, require analytics infrastructure, and produce reports nobody reads. This is 7 questions, takes 2 minutes, and produces actionable data you can discuss at any team or board meeting.

**Frequency:** Quarterly. More often creates survey fatigue. Less often misses the signal.

**Method:** Anonymous. Paper card, simple online form (Google Form), or even a show-of-hands at a gathering. The method should match your people’s comfort. If your volunteers don’t do email, don’t email a survey.

### The Seven Questions

|#|Question                                                                          |What It Measures      |Risk If Low                                                                                    |
|-|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1|I understand how my work contributes to our mission.                              |Purpose clarity       |Drift. People doing tasks without connection to why.                                           |
|2|I feel appreciated for what I do here.                                            |Recognition           |Resentment. Silent departure. “They don’t even notice.”                                        |
|3|I have what I need to do my job/role well.                                        |Resource adequacy     |Frustration. Workarounds that become permanent dysfunction.                                    |
|4|My opinions and ideas are heard.                                                  |Voice                 |Disengagement. People stop offering input and start going through motions.                     |
|5|I would recommend this organization to a friend who wanted to volunteer/work here.|Net promoter (loyalty)|Reputation risk. People won’t recruit their networks.                                          |
|6|I see myself still being involved with this organization in 12 months.            |Retention intent      |Flight risk. This is your early warning.                                                       |
|7|What is one thing we could do better?                                             |Open signal           |This is your suggestion box, your complaint line, and your innovation pipeline in one question.|

### Scoring (Questions 1–6)

Three-point scale. Not five. Not ten. Three. Small organizations don’t need statistical precision. They need signal.

|Agree ✔️                              |Neutral —                                    |Disagree ✖️                    |
|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
|This is fine. No intervention needed.|Ambiguous. Worth watching. Not yet a problem.|This is a signal. Investigate.|

### Reading the Results

**You don’t need analytics. You need pattern recognition.**

- **If Question 2 (Appreciation) is consistently low:** You have a recognition problem. It’s the cheapest thing to fix and the most common thing to neglect.
- **If Question 4 (Voice) is low:** People have stopped offering ideas because nothing happened when they did. The Suggestion Loop (Component 3) is the fix.
- **If Question 6 (12-month intent) drops:** You’re about to lose people. Run Stay Interviews (Component 2) immediately on your highest-value contributors.
- **If Question 5 (Recommend) is low but Question 6 (Stay) is high:** People like the work but not the experience. Something about the culture, leadership, or logistics is broken.
- **If everything is high:** Celebrate. Tell people. And run the Pulse again next quarter because things change.

**Question 7 (open-ended) goes directly into the Suggestion Loop.** Every response gets a visible acknowledgment. See Component 3.

## Component 2: Stay Interview

**What it replaces:** Exit interviews. The problem with exit interviews is obvious: by the time you’re conducting one, the person has already decided to leave. A stay interview asks the same questions while there’s still time to act on the answers.

**Frequency:** Annually for each person. More often for high-value contributors or people showing drift signals (declining hours, reduced enthusiasm, skipping events).

**Who conducts:** For employees: their direct supervisor. For volunteers: the coordinator or a trusted peer — someone the volunteer has a genuine relationship with. The interviewer should be someone the person will be honest with, not someone they feel they need to perform for.

**Setting:** Coffee, not conference room. Walking conversation, not clipboard. The formality should match the relationship. For volunteers, this can happen naturally at the end of a shift or event.

### The Five Stay Interview Questions

|#|Question                                                           |What You’re Learning                                                                                                                                   |
|-|-------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1|What do you look forward to when you come here?                    |What’s keeping them. This is your retention anchor. Protect it.                                                                                        |
|2|What are you learning here? Is there something you’d like to learn?|Growth. Even volunteers want to develop. If they’ve stopped learning, they’re coasting toward exit.                                                    |
|3|Do you feel your time here is well-used?                           |Efficiency and respect. If they feel their time is wasted, they’ll protect it by giving less of it.                                                    |
|4|Is there anything that frustrates you that we could fix?           |Friction. The thing they complain about at home but don’t mention at the office. Make it safe to say.                                                  |
|5|Is there anything that might cause you to step back or leave?      |The direct question. Most people will answer honestly if the conversation has been warm and genuine up to this point. If they deflect, that’s data too.|

### What to Do with the Answers

- **Question 1 (look forward to):** Whatever they name, don’t change it without warning. If someone says they love the sorting because it’s meditative, don’t move them without a conversation.
- **Question 2 (learning):** If they want to learn something, create the opportunity. Cross-training, shadowing, new responsibilities. This costs nothing and extends tenure.
- **Question 3 (time well-used):** If they say no, ask what would make it better. This often reveals process inefficiencies that affect everyone, not just this person.
- **Question 4 (frustration):** The test is not whether you fix it. The test is whether you acknowledge it, explain what’s possible, and follow up. Enter it into the Suggestion Loop.
- **Question 5 (might leave):** If they name something actionable, act on it. If they name something you can’t change (health, relocation, life stage), acknowledge it and plan for the transition using the Succession Planner.

**Critical rule:** Never conduct a stay interview if you’re not prepared to act on what you hear. Asking people what they need and then doing nothing is worse than never asking.

### Stay Interview Documentation

Keep notes brief and private. This is not a performance review. It’s a relationship maintenance tool.

|Field               |Content                                                           |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Person**          |[Name]                                                            |
|**Date**            |[Date]                                                            |
|**Interviewer**     |[Name]                                                            |
|**Retention Anchor**|What keeps them here (from Q1)                                    |
|**Growth Interest** |What they want to learn or do more of (from Q2)                   |
|**Friction Point**  |What frustrates them (from Q4) — entered into Suggestion Loop? Y/N|
|**Flight Risk**     |Low / Medium / High (from Q5 and overall tone)                    |
|**Action Committed**|What you promised to do, and by when                              |
|**Follow-up Date**  |[Date] — did you do what you said you’d do?                       |

## Component 3: Suggestion Loop

**What it replaces:** The suggestion box. The problem with suggestion boxes is not that people don’t have ideas. It’s that ideas go in and nothing visible comes out. The box becomes a symbol of being ignored. This tool is a closed loop: every suggestion gets a visible response.

**Frequency:** Ongoing. The channel is always open. Responses are batched and communicated monthly or quarterly.

**Method:** Whatever your people actually use. Google Form, text to a designated number, index card in a box, verbal to a coordinator. The barrier to entry should be as low as possible. Anonymous option must exist.

### The Loop (Four Steps)

|1. Receive                                      |2. Acknowledge                                                               |3. Route                                                                 |4. Close                                                                                                  |
|------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Suggestion enters through any channel. *Log it.*|Within 1 week: visible acknowledgment that it was received. *“We heard you.”*|Route to the person who can act on it. Apply Scale Test. *Who owns this?*|Communicate the outcome: adopted, modified, deferred, or declined (with reason). *“Here’s what happened.”*|

### Suggestion Log (Template)

|#|Suggestion|Date In|Routed To|Outcome|Date Closed|
|-|----------|-------|---------|-------|-----------|
|1|          |       |         |       |           |
|2|          |       |         |       |           |
|3|          |       |         |       |           |

### Outcome Categories

|Outcome     |What to Communicate                                                                                            |
|------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Adopted** |“We’re doing this. Here’s what it looks like and when.” Credit the suggester (if not anonymous).               |
|**Modified**|“We can’t do exactly this, but we took the idea and adapted it. Here’s what we’re doing instead.”              |
|**Deferred**|“Good idea, wrong time. We’re putting it on the list for [specific timeframe]. We haven’t forgotten.”          |
|**Declined**|“We considered this carefully. Here’s why we’re not moving forward: [honest reason]. Thank you for raising it.”|

**The most important outcome is Declined with a reason.** People don’t need every idea adopted. They need to know their idea was genuinely considered and that the “no” has a reason. A “no” with a reason builds more trust than silence.

### Closing the Loop Visibly

Once per quarter (or monthly for active organizations), share a summary of suggestions received and outcomes. This can be a standing agenda item, a section in a newsletter, a physical board showing suggestions and status, or a verbal report: “We received X suggestions this quarter. Here’s what happened.”

**The visible close is the whole point.** Without it, this is just another suggestion box.

## Component 4: Departure Debrief

**What it replaces:** Exit interviews. For employees, this follows traditional exit interview structure. For volunteers, it’s different — volunteers rarely give formal notice. They just stop showing up. The debrief has to account for both scenarios.

**When:** Within 2 weeks of departure (employees) or within 2 weeks of detecting absence (volunteers).

**Who conducts:** Someone the departing person trusts, not the person they’re leaving because of. For employees: HR or a senior leader, not the direct supervisor. For volunteers: a coordinator or peer they’re comfortable with.

### Detecting Volunteer Departure

Volunteers don’t submit resignations. Watch for these signals:

- Missed two consecutive scheduled shifts without explanation
- Declined three consecutive invitations to events or meetings
- Reduced hours or commitment without discussing it
- Withdrew from communication channels (stopped responding, left group chats)
- Said yes to something and then didn’t show

**When you detect these signals:** Reach out within one week. Not with guilt (“We missed you!”). With genuine care (“Hey, just checking in. How are you doing?”). The goal is to understand, not to retain. Sometimes people leave for good reasons. Learn from it.

### Departure Debrief Questions

|#|Question                                                |What You’re Learning                                                                                                        |
|-|--------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1|What originally drew you to this organization?          |What attracted them. Is your value proposition still working, or has it drifted from reality?                               |
|2|What was the best part of your experience here?         |Your strengths, from the inside. Protect these.                                                                             |
|3|What was the most frustrating part?                     |Friction that you didn’t catch or couldn’t fix. If this echoes Pulse Check or Stay Interview data, the pattern is confirmed.|
|4|What could we have done to keep you?                    |The actionable question. Sometimes the answer is “nothing” (life change). Sometimes it’s something you could have fixed.    |
|5|What should we tell the next person who takes your role?|Knowledge transfer in one question. This is the verbal version of the Intern Tool’s knowledge capture document.             |

### Departure Debrief Record

|Field                 |Content                                                                                                |
|----------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Person**            |[Name]                                                                                                 |
|**Role**              |[Role / function]                                                                                      |
|**Tenure**            |[Start date] – [End date] ([X] months/years)                                                           |
|**Departure Type**    |Voluntary / Involuntary / Fade-out (volunteer) / Life change                                           |
|**Retention Anchor**  |What kept them (Q1–Q2)                                                                                 |
|**Friction Point**    |What frustrated them (Q3)                                                                              |
|**Preventable?**      |Yes / No / Partially — what could we have done? (Q4)                                                   |
|**Knowledge Transfer**|Key information for successor (Q5)                                                                     |
|**Pattern Match**     |Does this departure echo themes from Pulse Checks, Stay Interviews, or other departures? Y/N — describe|

**Feed into Retention Diagnostic (Tool 39):** After 3+ departure debriefs, run Tool 39 to identify patterns. Are departures clustered by role, tenure, reporting relationship, or season?

## The Annual Cycle

|Quarter    |Component               |Action                                                      |Output                                                                |
|-----------|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Q1**     |Pulse Check             |Run 7-question pulse                                        |Baseline data. Identify any immediate red flags.                      |
|**Q1**     |Stay Interviews         |Schedule stay interviews for top 5–10 contributors          |Retention anchors identified. Flight risks flagged. Actions committed.|
|**Q2**     |Pulse Check             |Run 7-question pulse                                        |Compare to Q1. Trend emerging?                                        |
|**Q2**     |Suggestion Loop         |Close loop on all Q1–Q2 suggestions                         |Visible summary shared with all people.                               |
|**Q3**     |Pulse Check             |Run 7-question pulse                                        |Pre-season signal check (for seasonal organizations).                 |
|**Q3**     |Stay Interview follow-up|Did you do what you said you’d do in Q1?                    |Accountability. Trust maintenance.                                    |
|**Q4**     |Pulse Check             |Run 7-question pulse (annual comparison)                    |Full-year trend. Board/leadership report.                             |

|**Ongoing**|Suggestion Loop         |Channel always open. Responses visible.                     |Trust that ideas are heard and considered.                            |
|**Ongoing**|Departure Debrief       |Within 2 weeks of any departure                             |Knowledge preserved. Patterns tracked.                                |

## Quick Implementation Guide

**If you can only do one thing:** Run the Pulse Check once. Seven questions. Two minutes per person. You’ll learn more from one pulse than from a year of assumptions.

**If you can do two things:** Add Stay Interviews for your top five contributors. The people you can’t afford to lose should hear from you before they decide to leave.

**If you can do three things:** Add the Suggestion Loop. Not the channel — you probably already have one. Add the visible close. Tell people what happened to their ideas.

**If you can do all four:** You have a complete people intelligence system that most large organizations struggle to maintain. Run the annual cycle. Feed the data into Tool 39 (Retention Diagnostic) and FP-3 (Volunteer Fragility Map). You’ll see problems coming before they arrive.

## System Relationships

|Component                                |Relationship                                                                                                                                                                                        |
|-----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Retention Diagnostic (Tool 39)**       |The analytical layer. Departure Debriefs feed raw data into Tool 39. After 3+ departures, Tool 39 identifies whether the pattern is structural, cultural, or incidental.                            |
|**Volunteer Fragility Map (FP-3)**       |The operational layer. Stay Interviews identify flight risks. FP-3 maps the operational impact if those flight risks materialize. Together they answer: who might leave, and what breaks if they do?|
|**Key-Person Dependency Detector (FP-7)**|If a Stay Interview reveals a high flight risk on a key person, FP-7 maps the dependency and initiates cross-training before departure.                                                             |
|**Mission Drift Detector (Tool 31)**     |If Pulse Check Question 1 (purpose clarity) scores low across multiple people, Tool 31 diagnoses whether the mission has drifted or just isn’t being communicated.                                  |
|**Stakeholder Translator (Tool 30)**     |Pulse Check and Stay Interview findings may need to be communicated to the board, funders, or community. Tool 30 ensures the message is translated appropriately for each audience.                 |
|**Talent Calibration Grid**              |Stay Interview data informs the 9-Box assessment. A high-performing contributor who is also a high flight risk needs a different intervention than one who is deeply anchored.                      |
|**Board Decision Scaffold (Tool 34)**    |Pulse Check and Suggestion Loop data may surface issues that require governance decisions. Route through Tool 34 before acting.                                                                     |
